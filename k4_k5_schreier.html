Help & Reference

What is this?

This visualizes the associahedron Kn+1 (a.k.a. Stasheff polytope) embedded inside the Schreier coset graph Gn = S2n / (Sn×Sn).

The C(2n,n) vertices are binary strings with n ones and n zeros — cosets of the symmetric group. The Cn (Catalan number) red vertices are Dyck paths: binary strings where every prefix has at least as many 1s as 0s. Each Dyck path bijects to a full parenthesization of (n+1) symbols and to a binary tree with n internal nodes.

Edge Types

━━ Solid red Tamari ∩ Schreier: a single tree rotation (A·B)·C → A·(B·C) that also happens to be an adjacent transposition on the binary string. These are edges of both the associahedron and the Schreier graph.
┈┈ Green dotted Rotation-only: a valid tree rotation that moves bits across multiple positions in the binary string. These are real associahedron edges that "jump" through the Schreier graph — invisible to adjacent transpositions.
┈┈ Orange dashed Adj-trans ≠ Tamari: an adjacent transposition connecting two Dyck paths, but not a valid tree rotation. These are "impostor" edges — they exist in the Schreier graph but don't belong to the associahedron.
── Dim gray Schreier edges involving at least one non-Dyck vertex. These form the background lattice of the coset graph.

Why K₄ vs K₅?

K₄ (n=3): The pentagon — 5 vertices, 5 edges. Here only 1 edge is rotation-only and 1 is impostor. Since both edge sets produce 5-edge pentagons, they're accidentally isomorphic, which masked the error for years.

K₅ (n=4): 14 vertices, 21 edges. Now 6 edges differ between the two constructions. The associahedron is 3-regular (every vertex has degree 3) while the Schreier Dyck subgraph is not. This is where the error becomes visible.
